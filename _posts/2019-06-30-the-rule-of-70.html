The rule of 70 is a quick way to approximate doubling time for an exponentially growing quantity. It says:

A quantity with x percent annual growth will take 70/x years to double.
On first glance, this approximation should only be accurate for a particular range of x. Below is a simple interactive diagram demonstrating the accuracy of this approximation.

The approximation is surprisingly good. (I was expecting something worse, which is why I began this post to begin with!)

Let's check this outcome mathematically. The exact formula for doubling time given x% annual growth is given by $$\frac{\log 2}{\log(1 + x/100)}.$$ Approximating log 2 by 0.7 and using the linear approximation $$\log(1 + y) \approx y,$$ we obtain precisely the rule of 70. The approximation is valid essentially for as long as the linear approximation for log holds. Essentially, x/100 << 1 suffices.
