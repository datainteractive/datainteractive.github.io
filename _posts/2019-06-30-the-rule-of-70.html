The rule of 70 is a quick way to approximate doubling time for an exponentially growing quantity. It says:

A quantity with x percent annual growth will take 70/x years to double.
On first glance, this is only accurate for a particular range of x. Below is a simple interactive diagram displaying the accuracy of this approximation.

Evidently, the approximation is surprisingly good. The exact formula for doubling time is given by $\frac{\log 2}{\log( 1 + x/100)}.$
